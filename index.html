Jay Kang

I am an Assistant Professor at the Institute of Economic Research, Kyoto University. My research studies human capital, family investment, education policy, and inequality.

Labor Economics · Family Economics · Structural Estimation · Economics of Education

I combine survey and administrative data with experimental, quasi-experimental, and structural methods to study how families and students make educational investments, and how institutions shape inequality across the life cycle.
