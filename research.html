Research

Human capital, family investment, and inequality.

My work uses applied microeconomic methods and structural models to understand education choices, household allocation, and policy design.

Working papers

Dynamic Competition in Parental Investment and Child's Efforts

Jay Kang

This paper develops and estimates a dynamic tournament model of educational investment in South Korea. The model separates direct human-capital returns from competition incentives generated by scarce college seats.

Sibling Spillovers in Human Capital Accumulation

with Felipe Barrera-Osorio, Leonardo Bonilla-Mejía, Matias Busso, Sebastian Galiani, Juan Muñoz-Morales, and Juan Pantano

This paper studies how education transfers targeted to one child affect siblings and household allocation. It combines randomized policy variation with a structural model of family investment.
